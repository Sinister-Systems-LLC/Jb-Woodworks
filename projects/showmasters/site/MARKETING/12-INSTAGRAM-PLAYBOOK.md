## Posting cadence

### Feed posts: 2× per week (Tuesday + Friday)

**Tuesday 11am local time:** rotating content type
- Week 1: 3-photo carousel from a recent show
- Week 2: Crew member profile (photo + name + role + years)
- Week 3: 5-slide educational carousel
- Week 4: Behind-the-scenes from a named show (with client permission)

**Friday 1pm local time:** the "art shot"
- Single high-quality photo from a recent show. Cinematic. Bold caption.

### Reels: 2× per week (Wednesday + Saturday)

**Wednesday 7pm:** time-lapse or transformation
- Load-in time-lapse, before/after stage setup, truck unpack to deck-ready.

**Saturday 8pm:** behind-the-scenes moment
- A specific crew action — a rigger going up the truss, the moment doors open, the load-out adrenaline.

### Stories: 3–5× per week (daily during show weeks)

- Behind-the-scenes from the day.
- Polls + questions ("What's the worst load-in time you've had?").
- Repost crew tagging the company.
- Quick "we're crewing [show] this weekend" announcements.

### Highlights (Instagram's pinned Stories above the feed)

Set up 5 highlights, in this order:

1. **The Crew** — portraits of team members
2. **Shows** — by event type / venue
3. **Behind the Scenes** — load-ins, load-outs, truss work
4. **The Why** — quick explanations of W-2, insurance, Crew Lead model
5. **Careers** — apply to be on the crew

Cover images: a consistent SMPL-brand graphic (single black square with gold corner triangle + label).

## Reels — the most important format

Instagram's algorithm pushes Reels harder than feed posts. A single great Reel can outperform 20 feed posts in reach.

### What a good SMPL Reel looks like

- **Length:** 9–15 seconds optimal. 30 max.
- **Vertical:** 9:16 aspect ratio (1080×1920).
- **Native captions:** burn captions in, large + readable. Don't rely on the auto-caption.
- **Sound:** real on-site audio (truss rattles, crew calls, lighting hum) OR a trending audio track with attribution. Never silent.
- **Hook in first 1 second:** stage in motion, first cue firing, crew member doing something interesting.
- **Watermark:** subtle SMPL mark in a corner — `BRANDING/marks/02-mark-rounded-square.svg` at ~80px, 70% opacity.

### Editing in CapCut (the basics)

CapCut is free and does everything we need.

1. Import 4–8 short clips.
2. Trim each to 1–3 seconds.
3. Apply the SMPL color grade: bump shadows down, push gold/orange tones, slight contrast lift.
4. Add captions in a sans-serif, bold, white-on-shadow.
5. Pick a sound — either a low-key trending track OR keep the natural audio.
6. Add the SMPL watermark mark in the bottom-right corner.
7. Export 1080×1920, 30 fps.

Save the project file. We may need to remix later.

## Captions — how to write the body

Two-paragraph maximum. The first sentence is the hook.

### Template

> **[Hook — what happened, 6–10 words]**
>
> [2–4 sentences of context — the show, the crew, the moment.]
>
> [Optional: a CTA or quiet flex.]
>
> ---
> #stagehand #eventcrew #orlando #liveevents #behindthescenes

### Examples

**Good:**

> 14-hour load-in. Doors at 7. Crew at 5.
>
> The corporate plenary at Orange County Convention Center this week — 38 hands on deck, four trucks of gear, one Crew Lead running the show. We crewed it without a single change order.
>
> #stagehand #orlando #liveevents #occc #corporateevent

**Bad:**

> Big week for the team! Such an amazing show. Crew crushed it. So proud!! 💪🎉🎤

(Why bad: no specifics, generic adjectives, emojis, no context.)

## Stories — the daily heartbeat

Stories are where SMPL feels alive. The point isn't quality — it's frequency and human-ness.

### What to post in Stories

- 5am dock arrival ("Show day. Orlando.")
- Coffee + sunrise shot
- A close-up of something interesting on-site (a gear case, a truss being lifted, a stage being built)
- A poll: "Best venue in Orlando: OCCC vs. Amway?"
- A repost when a crew member tags @showmastersproductionlogistics
- A quick "doors at 7" announcement
- A post-show "and we're done" with the dark stage
- A meta moment: the strike in time-lapse

### Stories DON'T need to be polished

A 3-second clip of the crew loading a truck is fine. Producers value seeing the real work.

### Use stickers strategically

- **Poll sticker** — engagement is enormous, and IG rewards accounts that use it.
- **Question sticker** — opens DMs. Repost good Q&A as a follow-up.
- **Location sticker** — boosts local discoverability.
- **Mention sticker** when crew tags us — boosts the crew's engagement and ours.
- **Music sticker** — instant boost in Stories reach.

### Stickers we DON'T use

- Emoji-only reactions.
- "Add yours" templates (off-brand).
- GIPHY GIFs in Stories (looks cheap).

## DMs — the inbound channel

When inquiries come in via DM, the response rule:

- **<15 minutes** during business hours. (Same urgency as the form.)
- **Greet by name** + acknowledge what they said.
- **Move it to email/phone** for anything beyond "what's your hourly rate?": "Easiest if you reach Orders@ShowMasters.com or (877) 765-2267 — I'll loop in the right project lead."

## Boosting / paid promotion — when to spend

Wait until month 3 of consistent organic posting. Then experiment with $50 boosts on the highest-performing organic post per month.

Boost only on:
- A post that's already getting 3× our normal engagement (proven content).
- Target audience: location radius 100 miles around Orlando OR Dallas; interests = "event production," "trade shows," "live entertainment"; ages 28–55.
- Goal: profile visits + DMs (not video views).

If $50 generates 2+ DMs or 10+ profile visits, scale to $150. If not, pause and rethink.

Cap: $300/month on Instagram ads. Don't exceed without operator approval.

## Algorithm — what we know

Instagram's algorithm (as of 2026) weighs:

1. **Saves** (highest signal) — content people save to view later. "Educational" carousels and "Know-how" posts get saved.
2. **Shares** — content people DM to friends. "Behind-the-scenes" Reels get shared.
3. **Comments** — genuine ones, not bot spam.
4. **Watch time** on Reels — first 3 seconds must hook.
5. **Profile visits** — the post made someone curious enough to look at our profile.

Likes are nearly worthless. Save-worthy and share-worthy content is what wins.

## When things go sideways

### Account hacked

- Activate Instagram's recovery flow at help.instagram.com.
- Have 2FA on at all times (use an authenticator app, NOT SMS — SIM-swap risk).
- Operator + agency lead are the only people with login. No team logins.

### A bad review or accusation in DMs

- Don't argue publicly.
- Respond once, factually: "Hi — happy to look into this. Please email Orders@ShowMasters.com so we can investigate properly."
- Move it offline.

### Crew member posts something off-brand from the company account

- Pull the account back to operator + agency lead only.
- Internal-only review of the post.
- Public correction only if the original post was harmful; otherwise let it pass.
