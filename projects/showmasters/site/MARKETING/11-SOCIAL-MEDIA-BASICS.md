# 11 — Social Media Basics

This is the simple-English starting point for SMPL on social. If you've never run a business social account before, read this first. The platform-specific deep dives are in docs 12 (Instagram) and 13 (TikTok).

Goal: spend under 4 hours per week on social, and convert it into 2–4 inbound inquiries per month within 6 months.

---

## Why does an event-crew company need social media?

Three reasons:

1. **Recruitment.** Half of the crew we hire find us on Instagram or TikTok. Posting our work attracts skilled riggers, stagehands, and techs.
2. **Trust.** Producers who Google us also check our Instagram. An empty or inactive account looks suspicious. An active one with crew on shows looks legitimate.
3. **Top of mind.** Producers book SMPL when they remember SMPL. Social keeps us in their feed between bookings.

We are NOT doing social media to "go viral" or get a million followers. We're doing it to:
- Get 8–15 inbound inquiries per month from social by month 6.
- Keep our recruiting funnel full.
- Stay credible as a real, operating business.

---

## Which platforms (and which to skip)

In priority order. Don't add a platform until the previous one is running for 90 days consistently.

### Tier 1 — start here

| Platform | Why it matters for us |
|---|---|
| **Instagram** | The producers + crew watch. Highest ROI per hour of effort. Reels reach is enormous. |
| **Google Business Profile posts** | Counts as "social" — see `03-GMB-RANKING.md`. Free local-pack boost. |

### Tier 2 — add at month 3

| Platform | Why |
|---|---|
| **LinkedIn (company page)** | The decision-makers (producers, directors, venue ops) read LinkedIn. Higher-quality leads, lower volume. |
| **TikTok** | Reach is unmatched in 2026. Even B2B audiences find brands here. Recruiting tool especially. See doc 13. |

### Tier 3 — add at month 6, only if Tier 1+2 are consistent

| Platform | Why |
|---|---|
| **YouTube** | Long-form content lives forever. SEO bonus (YouTube ranks in Google search). |
| **Facebook page** | Older venue ops + house-of-worship contacts still use it. Auto-cross-post from Instagram. |

### Platforms we DO NOT use

- **X / Twitter** — wrong audience, low ROI for B2B event labor. Skip.
- **Threads / Bluesky / Mastodon** — too early; revisit in 2027.
- **Pinterest** — wrong audience.
- **Snapchat** — wrong audience.
- **Reddit** — only as a consumer (for industry forums), never as a brand.

## Brand voice (the short version)

How SMPL sounds on social:

- **Plain English.** No corporate-speak. No "we are excited to announce." Talk like a person.
- **Specific numbers.** "14-hour load-in" beats "long day." "38 crew" beats "huge team."
- **The work, not the marketing.** Show. Don't tell about showing.
- **Crew-first.** When in doubt, talk about the team, not the company.
- **Never:**
  - No emojis. None.
  - No "we crushed it" / "amazing job team" / motivational-poster language.
  - No politics, ever.
  - No stock photos. Use our own footage.
  - No AI-generated visuals presented as real (until nano banana integration is wired and operator approves a specific use).

---

## What to post — the 5 content types

Every post fits one of these. If yours doesn't, don't post it.

1. **The work** — photo or short video from a real show. Load-in, rig, run, strike.
2. **The crew** — a portrait of a real SMPL team member with their name, role, and years on the team.
3. **The know-how** — a tip, fact, or explanation about the industry. "What's the difference between a stagehand and a tech?"
4. **The wins** — behind-the-scenes from a named show (with client permission).
5. **The why** — values content. Why W-2. Why insurance is in the rate. Why the same Crew Lead every show.

Mix: about 40% Work, 20% Crew, 20% Know-how, 10% Wins, 10% Why.

---

## How often to post

Keep this realistic and sustainable.

| Platform | Frequency | Best days/times |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram feed | 2× per week | Tue + Fri, 10am–2pm local |
| Instagram Reels | 2× per week | Wed + Sat, evening (7–9pm) |
| Instagram Stories | 3–5× per week | Daily during show weeks |
| TikTok | 3× per week | Mon, Thu, Sat — evening |
| LinkedIn | 2× per week | Tue + Thu, 8–10am |
| GMB | 1× per week | Monday morning |
| Facebook | Auto-cross-post from Instagram (Buffer / Later) | n/a |

If you fall behind, prioritize **Instagram Reels + GMB posts** above all else. Those two drive the most inbound inquiry per hour invested.

## Tools (free or cheap)

| Tool | Use | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Phone camera | Primary shooting tool | Already own |
| CapCut Desktop or Mobile | Video editing for Reels + TikTok | Free |
| Canva | Quick graphics when needed | Free / $13/mo for Pro |
| Buffer or Later | Schedule posts, cross-platform | $15–$25/mo |

**Skip:**
- Hootsuite ($99/mo and bloated).
- Sprout Social ($249/mo, enterprise overkill).
- Any "AI social media generator" that promises "100 posts a month." Slop.

## Hashtags — the simple rule

Use 5–10 hashtags per Instagram post (no more — diminishing returns). Mix three types:

1. **Industry hashtags** (general): `#stagehand`, `#eventcrew`, `#liveevents`, `#productioncrew`, `#rigger`
2. **Local hashtags** (specific): `#orlandoevents`, `#fortwortheventcrew`, `#orlandoproduction`, `#dfwevents`
3. **Show-type hashtags**: `#tradeshow`, `#concertcrew`, `#corporateevent`, `#festivalcrew`

On TikTok, use 3–5 hashtags max, weighted toward broader reach: `#behindthescenes`, `#dayinmylife`, `#liveevents`.

On LinkedIn, hashtags barely matter. Use 2–3 broadest only.

## The first 90 days — a step-by-step

### Days 1–7: Audit + setup

- Audit existing accounts. What's posted? When was the last post? Is the bio up to date?
- Make sure every profile has:
  - The same `pfp-square.svg` exported as PNG (1024×1024).
  - The same one-paragraph bio with phone + website.
  - Link in bio to `showmasters.com/links` (build this page on the site — see Doc 12).
  - "Event Production Service" category.
- Connect Instagram + Facebook so cross-posting works.

### Days 8–21: Build a 30-post backlog

Spend one Saturday capturing content from the next week's shows. Aim for:
- 30 photo-and-caption posts ready to schedule.
- 10 Reels-ready video clips.
- 5 Stories templates (polls, questions, behind-the-scenes).

### Days 22–90: Run the cadence

Post 2× per week on Instagram + 1× per week on GMB. Don't add platforms yet.

After 30 days of consistent posting, add LinkedIn. After 60 days, add TikTok.

By day 90, you should have:
- 30+ feed posts, 25+ Reels, 50+ Stories on Instagram.
- 12+ posts each on LinkedIn + GMB.
- 12+ TikTok videos.

## How to know if it's working

Three numbers, reviewed at day 30, 60, and 90:

1. **Follower growth** — is it climbing? Even slowly is fine. (Target: +50/month on IG in months 1–3.)
2. **Engagement rate** — likes + comments + saves ÷ followers. Target 3%+ on Instagram.
3. **Direct inquiries citing social** — when the contact form's "how did you hear about us?" mentions Instagram, TikTok, or LinkedIn. Target: 1+/month by day 60, 4+/month by day 180.

If follower growth is happening but inquiries aren't, the content is too entertaining and not converting. Shift toward more "Why" and "Know-how" posts.

If inquiries grow but follower count is flat, we're getting bookings from existing reach. Add a platform.

---

## What kills a social account (avoid these)

1. **Going silent for 3 weeks then posting 5 things in a day.** The algorithm penalizes inconsistency. Schedule a buffer; never run out.
2. **Posting about politics or current events.** One opinion costs us bookings. Stay neutral.
3. **Engagement-baiting.** "Tag a friend who needs this!" reads as desperate.
4. **Reposting competitors' content.** Just don't.
5. **Buying followers.** Instagram detects this within 90 days and downranks the entire account permanently.
6. **Overediting photos.** Crank the saturation too high and it looks fake. Our shots are dark + cinematic by nature — let the gold gradient lighting do the work.

---

## One last thing

The single highest-leverage post you can make this month is:

> A 60-second Reel showing a real load-in from time-lapse open to first cue. Caption: "14 hours from empty room to doors open. Orlando, this week." Hashtags: `#orlando #liveevents #stagehand #behindthescenes #showmasters`

If we make one of these per month for a year, the account compounds. Everything else is optimization.
