# 06 — Content Calendar

What to post, where, when. The point of content isn't to "be on social" — it's to keep showing up where producers look so we stay top of mind between bookings.

Keep it boring and repeatable. The compounding is the win.

## Content pillars (every post fits one of five)

Stick to these five. If a post idea doesn't fit, don't post it.

1. **The Work.** Photos and video from real shows — load-in, rig, run, strike. No staging required; we have crew on a site somewhere every day.
2. **The Crew.** People shots. "Meet [Name], Crew Lead — 12 years." Honors the team and humanizes the brand. Crew shares it. Their friends and family see it. Referrals follow.
3. **The Know-How.** Educational. "What's the difference between a stagehand and a tech?" "What does a rigger actually do?" These rank in search and get bookmarked.
4. **The Wins.** Behind-the-scenes of named shows we crewed (with client permission). Big names build trust.
5. **The Why.** Mission / values content. Why we're W-4. Why insurance matters. Why the same Crew Lead twice. This converts considerers into customers.

Rough mix: 40% The Work · 20% The Crew · 20% The Know-How · 10% The Wins · 10% The Why.

## Instagram: month-by-month feed plan

A repeating template. Same shape, different content each month.

**Tuesday feed post (alternating types):**
- Week 1 of month: A "Work in action" 3-photo carousel from a recent show.
- Week 2: A "Meet the crew" portrait + name + role + years with SMPL.
- Week 3: A "Know-how" carousel — 5-slide explainer ("What's a deck hand?").
- Week 4: A "Win" — behind-the-scenes from a named show (with client permission).

**Friday feed post:**
- Always a clean hero photo + a single sentence. The IG "art shot" of the week. No carousel.

**Wednesday + Saturday Reels:**
- 9–15 second clips. Load-in time-lapse, rigger up the truss, the moment doors open. Native captions. Trending audio when appropriate (rare; usually keep it natural).

**Stories (daily during show weeks):**
- Behind-the-scenes. Coffee at 5am, sunrise on the dock, the calm before doors. Polls and questions for engagement ("What's the worst load-in time you've ever had?").

## TikTok: what works for crewing (it's different from IG)

TikTok rewards specifics and behind-the-scenes shock value. Producers don't browse TikTok the way they browse LinkedIn — but their assistants, project managers, and crew do, and SMPL becomes a household name in the industry that way.

**Three posts per week, repeating types:**
- Monday: A "60-second show breakdown" — time-lapse of a load-in with captions explaining each step.
- Thursday: A "day in the life of a [role]" — POV with a rigger, a tech, a forklift operator.
- Saturday: A "tour case unboxing" or "how this thing works" — gear teach-back. (Yes, gear nerds are an audience.)

Hashtag template: `#stagehand #eventcrew #liveevents #behindthescenes #orlando` (or `#dallas`/`#fortworth`).

Deep details and platform-specific tactics in `13-TIKTOK-PLAYBOOK.md`.

## LinkedIn: who actually reads this

Producers. Production-company directors. Venue ops managers. Decision makers who don't watch TikTok. The audience that books $100k+ contracts.

**Two posts per week:**

- **Tuesday — Thought leadership.** A short essay (3–5 paragraphs) on something specific: "Why we pay riggers more than stagehands and why you should too." "The hidden cost of 1099 labor on your show." Posted from the company page AND boosted by the leadership team commenting / sharing.

- **Thursday — A win.** "Last week we crewed [show] at [venue] — 14 days, 38 crew, 0 incidents. Here's what made it work." With one photo (no carousel — LinkedIn buries them).

## Email newsletter (monthly, last Wednesday)

Sent to past clients + opt-in subscribers. Short. One screen.

**Template:**

- One-paragraph intro from leadership.
- One "show of the month" callout (photo + 3 sentences).
- One "Know-how" link (the blog post from this month).
- One "Behind the scenes" Reel or TikTok embedded.
- "Need crew for an upcoming show? Hit reply or call (877) 765-2267."

Tools: Buttondown or Mailcoach (self-hostable). Stay off Mailchimp — we own our list.

## YouTube long-form (monthly, third Thursday)

One ~6–10 minute video per month. Cheap to make if shot on phone + lavalier mic. Editing in DaVinci Resolve (free) or CapCut Desktop.

**Six suggested topics for the first 6 months:**

1. "A 14-hour load-in, in 8 minutes" (time-lapse + voiceover walkthrough).
2. "Meet the rigger" (interview-style profile).
3. "What's in a road case?" (gear tour).
4. "Stagehand vs technician — the on-show difference" (educational).
5. "How a Crew Lead actually runs a show" (over-shoulder POV).
6. "From load-in to load-out — a corporate plenary in one day" (timelapse + voiceover).

Each video gets its own thumbnail in the SMPL brand. Use the gold accent + bold Inter caption on a dark stage backdrop.

## What we DO NOT post

- Stock photos of generic crowds or stages. Use our own footage.
- AI-generated images of stagehands. We have real people.
- Recycled content from competitors.
- Anything political.
- "Motivational quotes" with the gold logo slapped on top.
- Anything that requires an emoji to make the joke land. Write a better joke.

## How to measure if any of this is working

Three numbers, reviewed monthly:

1. **Reach** — total followers + monthly impressions across IG, TikTok, LinkedIn. Growing month-over-month is the floor.
2. **Engagement** — % of impressions that result in a save, share, or DM. Target 3%+ on IG, 5%+ on TikTok.
3. **Inquiries that mention social or web** — from the form's "How did you hear about us?" field. Target: 10% of monthly inquiries cite social within 6 months.

If reach grows but inquiries don't, the content is entertaining but not converting. Adjust toward more "Why" and "Know-how" content.

If inquiries grow but reach doesn't, we're getting bookings from the people we already reached. Add more channels.
