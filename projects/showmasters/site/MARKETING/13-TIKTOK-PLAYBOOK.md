# 13 — TikTok Playbook

TikTok is the recruiting + visibility juggernaut. Producers don't browse it for vendors the way they browse LinkedIn — but their crew, their assistants, and their project managers do. Get on TikTok consistently and SMPL becomes a household name in the event-labor world. That converts into bookings six months later.

If you're new to TikTok, read this whole doc before posting. The platform punishes mistakes harshly in the first 30 days.

---

## Should an event-crew company even be on TikTok?

Yes — three reasons:

1. **Recruiting.** Skilled stagehands, riggers, and techs are watching TikTok. We can recruit directly from the platform — much cheaper than Indeed or LinkedIn job ads.
2. **Behind-the-scenes content is the platform's bread and butter.** "How is this stage built?" is exactly the kind of question TikTok rewards.
3. **Reach.** A single Reels-style video on TikTok can hit 50,000 views from zero followers. The reach-per-post is unmatched.

We are NOT trying to be a "viral creator." We're trying to be the most recognizable event-labor brand on the platform within 12 months.

## What makes a TikTok work (the platform-specific rules)

1. **First 1.5 seconds is everything.** If they don't stop scrolling, the video is dead. Hook hard.
2. **Captions burned in.** TikTok auto-caption is unreliable. Use CapCut to burn them in white-on-shadow.
3. **9:16 vertical only.** No exceptions.
4. **30–60 seconds is the sweet spot in 2026.** Too short (under 8 sec) doesn't get watch-time; too long (>90 sec) loses attention.
5. **Trending sounds matter.** Check the "For You" page weekly — if a sound is trending in our niche (behind-the-scenes / day-in-the-life), use it within 7 days of seeing it.
6. **One CTA at the end.** "Follow for more behind-the-scenes from real live events." That's it. Don't try to be clever.

## Captions on TikTok

Different from Instagram. Shorter, punchier, often question-led.

### Format

> Hook line (a question or a stat).
>
> Optional: 1 short sentence of context.
>
> Optional: 1 CTA.
>
> #tag #tag #tag

### Examples

> What does it take to build a stage in 4 hours?
>
> 12 stagehands. 1 Crew Lead. Two truckloads of truss.
>
> Follow for the full breakdown.
>
> #stagehand #orlando #behindthescenes #liveevents

> The hidden cost of 1099 stagehands.
>
> If a contractor gets hurt on your show, your event insurance is on the hook.
>
> #eventproduction #1099vsw2 #liveevents

## Hashtags (3–5 max per video)

TikTok rewards quality over quantity on hashtags. Pick from:

**Broad reach (use 2 per video):**
`#behindthescenes` `#dayinmylife` `#liveevents` `#eventproduction`

**Industry-specific (use 1–2 per video):**
`#stagehand` `#rigger` `#productioncrew` `#trussing` `#corporateevent`

**Local (use 1 per video):**
`#orlando` `#dallas` `#dfw` `#texasevents`

NEVER use:
- `#fyp` `#foryou` `#foryoupage` — the algorithm doesn't care; you're wasting hashtag slots.
- More than 5 total — diminishing returns and looks spammy.

## Trending sounds — when to use them

TikTok's algorithm boosts videos that use rising sounds. To find them:

1. Open TikTok → "Discover" → "Sounds" tab.
2. Filter by "trending in your niche" (or just scroll until you see a sound used on multiple behind-the-scenes videos).
3. If the sound has between 5,000 and 200,000 uses, it's in the sweet spot — rising, not yet saturated.
4. Build a video around the sound's energy. Sometimes the sound dictates the cut.

**Do NOT use:**
- Music we don't have license for in original audio (TikTok's library is licensed for use on the platform only; never download and reuse).
- Trending sounds that are political or controversial.

## Filming setup — the bare minimum

You don't need professional gear. You DO need:

- Phone with 4K capable camera (any iPhone 13+ or recent Android).
- A small phone gimbal (DJI Osmo Mobile 6, $150) for stable shots. Pays for itself in 2 weeks.
- A lavalier mic (Rode Wireless ME, $150) for clear audio during interviews.
- A USB-C hub if recording while charging.
- Good vertical phone tripod (anything $25 will do).

Total: ~$350 one-time, then you can film anything.

## Editing in CapCut

The same CapCut workflow from `12-INSTAGRAM-PLAYBOOK.md` applies. Specific TikTok adjustments:

1. Set canvas to 9:16, 1080×1920.
2. Music goes at -6 dB if it's background; -2 dB if it's the primary audio.
3. Captions positioned in the SAFE zone (top 25% or bottom 25% — middle is hidden by UI overlays).
4. SMPL watermark in upper-left corner (not bottom — TikTok's UI overlays the bottom area heavily).
5. Export as 1080×1920, 30 fps, MP4.

Don't bother with TikTok's native editor — CapCut beats it.

## Engagement — the daily 15 minutes

Set a 15-minute timer once a day to:

- Reply to every comment with a real reply (not just an emoji — type words).
- Save 3–5 trending sounds from your niche.
- Watch 5–10 competitor videos and see what's working in the space.
- Look at "Analytics" tab and note any video that's outperforming.

That 15-minute discipline compounds. Without it, the account stagnates.

## Going live (when ready)

After 60 days of consistent posting, you'll unlock TikTok Live (requires 1,000 followers in most regions).

Use TikTok Live for:
- **Live load-in / load-out walkthroughs** — pop on for 20–30 minutes during a slow stretch.
- **Q&A sessions** — once a month, "Ask a Crew Lead anything."
- **Recruitment events** — "We're hiring riggers. Come learn what the job looks like."

Lives push the regular account's distribution. Even a 25-viewer live boosts the next 5 posts' reach.

## What we DO NOT do on TikTok

1. **Dance challenges.** Off-brand.
2. **Lip-syncing.** Off-brand.
3. **"Stitch" or "Duet" with off-brand creators.** Stay close to industry / professional creators only.
4. **Engagement bait** ("Comment XYZ if you agree").
5. **Faking emotion or drama.** The audience can spot it.
6. **Showing crew faces without consent.** Get a signed waiver before any crew member appears clearly on camera; ask in writing for each show.

## Recruitment on TikTok — the secret weapon

The single highest-ROI thing TikTok does for SMPL:

When a video gets 50,000+ views, we get 5–10 DMs from "how do I get on this crew?" Skilled stagehands and riggers find us this way. Our crew bench grows organically without a single Indeed dollar spent.

To set this up:

1. **Pinned video on profile:** "We're hiring in Orlando + Dallas. Link in bio."
2. **Recruitment-tagged content monthly:** every 4th video, end with "We hire stagehands, riggers, and techs. Apply at showmasters.com/careers."
3. **Reply to all "how do I apply" DMs within 24 hours** with a direct link to `/careers`.

By month 6, this becomes a real recruitment channel. Track it.

---

## Algorithm health — staying out of TikTok jail

These behaviors get accounts shadow-banned (still show on your own profile, but invisible in For You):

- Posting more than 5 videos in 24 hours from a single account.
- Mass-following (>50 follows/hour).
- Using copyrighted music without TikTok's library.
- "Watermark from another platform" — if you post a video with the Instagram or YouTube watermark visible, the video gets buried.

Avoid all four. Post 1 video per day max. Use TikTok's native music library. Re-export from CapCut without other-platform watermarks.

## What "good" looks like at month 12

- 3,000+ followers (modest by influencer standards, huge for B2B event labor).
- 100+ videos posted.
- Average views: 5,000+ per video.
- Best-performing video: 100k+ views.
- DMs: 10–15 inbound per month (recruiting + a few producer inquiries).
- Recognition: at least 5 producers in FL/TX tell you "I saw you on TikTok."

If we hit those numbers, TikTok becomes our cheapest recruitment + brand-awareness channel. If we miss them, the content style is wrong — shift toward more "day in the life" and less "info dump."
