# Social Media Playbook — Show Masters Production Logistics

**Compiled:** 2026-05-23

---

## Quick wins (do this month)

1. **Lock down handles** on all platforms — Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook, YouTube, Twitter/X (defensive), Threads (defensive). Use `@showmastersproductionlogistics` as the canonical handle (already in use on IG/FB/LinkedIn — match it everywhere else).
2. **Post once a week on Instagram** for 90 days. One post. Same day, same time. Build the muscle of consistency before scale.
3. **Set up a simple cross-post tool** (Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite — free tier for 1 user). Saves 80% of the time vs posting natively to each platform.
4. **Stand up a content calendar** — even a Google Sheet works. 5 columns: Date, Channel, Topic, Owner, Status. Fill in 30 days ahead.
5. **Hire or assign a social lead** — even 10 hours/week. Social fails without ownership. The role can be inside or contracted; doesn't matter as long as it's *one* person.

## The audience question

Before any channel work, decide who you're talking to. Show Masters has **three** distinct audiences:

| Audience | What they want from social | Best channels |
|---|---|---|
| **Producers** (booking decision-makers) | Trust signals, behind-the-scenes from named shows, industry expertise | LinkedIn (primary), Instagram feed (secondary), email |
| **Crew** (current + prospective hires) | Pay info, culture, recruitment signals, day-in-the-life | TikTok, Instagram Reels, Facebook (regional groups) |
| **Industry peers** (other production companies, vendors, IATSE locals, venue ops) | Mutual referrals, gear/tech updates, professional respect | LinkedIn, Instagram, industry-publication comments |

If you're not sure which audience a post targets, you're posting to nobody. Tag each post in the calendar with one of the three.

## Content pillars (5)

Every post fits exactly one of these. If a post idea doesn't fit, don't post it.

1. **The Work.** Real photos/video from shows. Load-in, rig, run, strike. No staging required; we have crew somewhere every day. **40% of posts.**
2. **The Crew.** People. "Meet [Name], Crew Lead — 12 years." Humanizes the brand. Crew shares it. Their friends and family see it. Referrals follow. **20%.**
3. **The Know-How.** Educational. "What's the difference between a stagehand and a tech?" "What does a rigger actually do?" These get bookmarked, shared, and rank in search. **20%.**
4. **The Wins.** Behind-the-scenes of named shows we crewed (with client permission). Big names = trust. **10%.**
5. **The Why.** Mission / values content. Why we're W-4. Why insurance matters. Why the same Crew Lead twice. Converts considerers into customers. **10%.**

## Channel-by-channel

### Instagram (the foundation channel)

**Cadence:** 2 feed posts/week + 2 Reels/week + Stories daily during show weeks.

**Feed format (alternating Tuesdays):**
- Week 1: "Work in action" 3-photo carousel from a recent show
- Week 2: "Meet the crew" portrait + name + role + years with SMPL
- Week 3: "Know-how" 5-slide explainer carousel
- Week 4: "Win" behind-the-scenes from a named show

**Friday post:** Always a clean hero photo + single sentence. The art shot of the week. No carousel.

**Reels (Weds + Sat):** 9-15 seconds. Load-in time-lapse, rigger up the truss, the moment doors open. Native captions. Trending audio sparingly (rare; usually keep natural).

**Stories (daily during show weeks):** Behind-the-scenes. Coffee at 5am, sunrise on the dock, the calm before doors. Polls/questions for engagement.

**Hashtag set (use 8-12 per post):** `#stagehand #eventcrew #liveevents #behindthescenes #orlando #dallas #stagecrew #productioncrew #showcall #riggers #lightingdesigner #eventproduction`

### TikTok (the recruitment channel)

TikTok rewards specifics and behind-the-scenes shock value. Producers don't browse TikTok the way they browse LinkedIn — but their assistants, project managers, and **crew** do. SMPL becomes a household name in the industry through TikTok.

**Cadence:** 3 posts/week (Mon/Thu/Sat).

**Repeating format:**
- **Monday:** "60-second show breakdown" — time-lapse of a load-in with captions explaining each step.
- **Thursday:** "Day in the life of a [role]" — POV with a rigger, tech, forklift operator.
- **Saturday:** "Tour case unboxing" or "How this thing works" — gear teach-back. Gear nerds are a real audience.

**Hashtags:** `#stagehand #eventcrew #liveevents #behindthescenes #orlandojobs` (or `#dallasjobs`/`#texasjobs`)

**Posting tip:** TikTok rewards posting consistency over peak quality. A workmanlike daily post beats a polished weekly one. Don't overthink.

### LinkedIn (the producer channel)

LinkedIn is where producers, production-company directors, venue ops managers, and decision makers spend professional time. Books $100k+ contracts. The audience is small but the conversion value per follower is the highest of any channel.

**Cadence:** 2 posts/week (Tue + Thu).

- **Tuesday — Thought leadership.** 3-5 paragraph essay on something specific: "Why we pay riggers more than stagehands." "The hidden cost of 1099 labor on your show." "What a real Crew Lead does at hour 10." Posted from the company page **AND boosted by the leadership team commenting/sharing**.
- **Thursday — A win.** "Last week we crewed [show] at [venue] — 14 days, 38 crew, 0 incidents. Here's what made it work." One photo (no carousel; LinkedIn buries them).

**Don't:** Use LinkedIn the way you use Instagram. Don't post art shots, don't use trending audio. LinkedIn is the most "professional" surface — keep it dry and useful.

### Facebook (the wide-net channel)

Facebook still matters for:
- Local-business search (especially regional venue operators, church AV ministries, school audio departments)
- Regional groups (FL/TX event-pro groups, AV-installer groups)
- Older producer demographics

**Cadence:** 2 posts/week, mostly mirrored from Instagram via auto-cross-post. Plus one regional-group post per week (different content depending on the group).

**Don't bother:** With Facebook ads here, unless a campaign-specific reason exists. The ROAS on Facebook ads for B2B services is generally poor.

### YouTube (the long-form channel)

**Cadence:** 1 long-form video per month. The format is intentional — quality over quantity. YouTube videos compound for years; Instagram posts decay in days.

**Monthly format options:**
- "Behind the scenes: [Named Show]" 5-8 minute mini-doc
- "How we crew a [show type]" 8-12 minute walkthrough
- "Crew Lead Q&A" 10-15 minute conversational format
- "Day in the life of a [role]" 8-10 minute POV
- "Show Masters annual review" once per year, 12-15 minutes

**Why this matters:** A producer searching "behind the scenes [venue] event crew" on YouTube finds your video. Watches it for 4 minutes. Now they know your company exists, your standards, your aesthetic, and your tone. This is the deepest top-of-funnel content available — every other channel is shallow by comparison.

### Email newsletter (the warm-list channel)

Monthly, last Wednesday of the month. Sent to past clients + opt-in subscribers.

**Template:**
- One-paragraph intro from leadership
- One "What we crewed this month" (with photo)
- One "Field notes" (link to the month's blog post)
- One "Hiring" callout (if applicable)
- One CTA — usually "Have a show coming up? Drop us a brief."

**Tooling:** Mailchimp free tier handles up to 500 contacts. Beehiiv free tier up to 2,500.

## Tools

Minimum stack:

- **Buffer** (free for 1 user, paid for more) — cross-posting + scheduling
- **Canva Pro** ($120/yr) — template-driven graphics for non-designers
- **CapCut** (free) — short-form video editing on phone or desktop
- **Lightroom mobile** (free or $5/mo) — phone-photo color grade
- **Mailchimp or Beehiiv** — newsletter

Total monthly spend: $5-25, depending on which tier you pick.

## What success looks like at 90 days

- Instagram: 500-1,500 new followers, 5%+ engagement on feed posts
- TikTok: 1,000-3,000 followers (broader top-of-funnel), 1-2 viral hits
- LinkedIn: 50-150 follower growth on the company page, 1+ inbound producer DM per week
- Email: 100-300 newsletter subscribers, 30%+ open rate
- Google Business Profile: 5+ new reviews, 100+ profile views/month

At 180 days, the metrics roughly double if cadence holds. At 365 days, social becomes a measurable lead-source channel — 5-15% of new producer leads will cite "saw you on Instagram/LinkedIn/TikTok" as part of how they found the company.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't buy followers.** Bots get culled, your engagement rate cratters, and your real audience leaves.
- **Don't post the same content cross-channel verbatim.** Each platform's audience expects native context.
- **Don't post about politics or current events** that don't directly relate to the live-event industry. Risk-adjusted return is negative.
- **Don't try to run all six channels simultaneously from day one.** Master Instagram + LinkedIn first. Add TikTok at day 60. Add YouTube at day 120. Add the rest as time/budget allows.
- **Don't chase virality.** Consistency beats virality for B2B services. A reliable feed of 200 engaged followers is worth more than 50,000 disengaged ones.
